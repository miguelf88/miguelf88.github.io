For a transit agency, a rideshed represents the geographic location from which passengers could access their service. For PART, this includes park & ride lots, transit terminals, or bus stops. The purpose of this project was to delineate the market areas for the PART system. A Park & Ride survey completed in mid-2018 helped me accomplish this task. I utilized a free geocoder from Texas A&M University and I plotted the home location of PART's passengers by which park & ride facility they parked their car. The results were circular shapes around urban park & ride facilities while more rural lots were fan shaped. What we learned from this is that because urban lots are more central, they attract passengers from a wider area while the rural lots primarily had passengers coming from upstream. In the survery results there were very few examples of passengers traveling upstream to access a park & lot.

The next step was to analyze the demographics for the rideshed. For this, I located tables from the 2013-2017 5-year estimates of the American Community Survey using American Fact Finder. I brought the Census data into ArcGIS for geoprocessing. I then create a dashboard to display the demographpic data.

When route planning, it is important to look at origin-destination data so the planner knows where people are coming from and going. OnTheMap is a great tool to use and download OD data from.
